Our Mission

What Is CommHelp?

CommHelp is a community-powered initiative to help Lower Manhattan residents start, grow, and sustain small businesses — through free workshops, mentorship, networking, and micro-grants.

We believe the best ideas for strengthening neighborhoods come from the people who live in them. Lower Manhattan is full of aspiring entrepreneurs — food vendors, creatives, freelancers, side-hustlers — who just need the right support to turn their talents into thriving businesses.

Powered by ioby ("In Our Backyards"), a nonprofit crowdfunding platform with an 87% success rate, we're raising community funds to provide:

  • 📚 Free business workshops (planning, permits, marketing, finance)
  • 🤝 1-on-1 mentorship with experienced local entrepreneurs
  • 🌐 Networking events connecting aspiring and established business owners
  • 💰 Micro-grants for the most promising business plans
  • 📖 Resource guides for NYC permits, licensing, and funding sources

🌿 What is ioby?

ioby stands for "In Our Backyards" — a nonprofit crowdfunding platform that helps neighborhood leaders plan, fund, and implement community projects.

  • 🎓 Free 1-on-1 coaching from a fundraising strategist
  • 📋 Fiscal sponsorship — accept tax-deductible donations without being a nonprofit
  • 💰 Match funding that can double or triple your donations
  • 🤝 Flexible funding — keep whatever you raise
  • 🏘️ 65% of leaders served are BIPOC

Step by Step

How to Start Your Own Project

1

Define Your Idea

Talk to 5–10 local business owners about their biggest challenges.

2

Build Your Team

Recruit 2–3 committed co-leaders. Solo campaigns rarely succeed.

3

Submit Your Idea

Go to ioby.org/idea. Within 24 hours, a staff member will contact you.

4

Get Your Free Coach

ioby assigns you a "Success Strategist" for free 1-on-1 coaching.

5

Map Your Donors

Fill out ioby's Prospect Chart: who you know, what they can give, who will ask.

6

Build Your Campaign Page

Tell your story with photos, videos, budget, and timeline.

7

Launch & Hustle

Aim for 30% funded in the first 48 hours — momentum matters!

8

Leverage Your Success

Use your funded campaign to apply for larger grants.

Practical Advice

Tips for Building Community Involvement

🎯 Start Where People Are

Don't assume — ask. Canvas local businesses and hold listening sessions.

💬 Make It Personal

People donate to people. Feature real stories of local entrepreneurs.

👥 Build a Team

Teams raise 3–5x more. Recruit business owners and community leaders.

📅 Host Monthly Events

Pop-up workshops and networking nights build momentum.

📱 Use Social Media

Shareable Canva graphics, donor shout-outs, progress updates.

💵 Ask Small, Think Big

$25 from 100 people = $2,500. Small donations add up fast.

🔄 Update Donors

Share milestones. Transparency builds trust and momentum.

🏛️ Partner Locally

BIDs, SCORE NYC, NYC SBS, community boards, local institutions.

🙋 Volunteer Roles

Mentors, event helpers, and social media sharers all count.

The Plan

From Idea to Sustainable Program

Phase 1

Pilot Months 1–3

6 free workshops for 20–30 aspiring entrepreneurs: business planning, permits, accounting, digital marketing, branding, funding.

💰 ioby crowdfunding + match grants

Phase 2

Expand Months 4–6

Add mentorship, networking events, resource directory. Launch micro-grant program.

💰 NYC SBS grants, CDFIs, corporate sponsors

Phase 3

Formalize Months 6–12

Establish as nonprofit or partner with existing org. Ongoing programming calendar.

💰 Grants, sponsorships, workshop fees

Phase 4

Scale Year 2+

Expand to other neighborhoods. Train graduates as mentors. Annual entrepreneur showcase.

💰 Self-sustaining diversified revenue
